# Placement fee revenue model

EPHotspot pays Operators a fixed fee to host Nodes at their location. All revenue from data sales flows to EPHotspot centrally. Operators are location partners, not resellers.

The alternative — Operators selling data and paying EPHotspot a subscription or revenue share — was rejected because it creates misaligned incentives: Operators on capped internet connections would lose money when users spend data purchased elsewhere on their network, and Operators would need to actively manage pricing and revenue, adding operational complexity they didn't sign up for.

The placement fee model means Operators never worry about who connects or where data was purchased. EPHotspot controls all pricing and billing. The global Data Balance per User is a direct consequence of this model — it is EPHotspot's network, not a collection of separate operator networks.
